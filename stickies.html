Stickys

The default sticky is indigo with white text and slightly rounded edges.

Use stickys to add additional styling to an important block of text, or to alert the user with a message.

Stickys are not recommended for call-to-action elements. Instead, use a button or a tag.

Customizing

Change the default background color.

class="red, gold, green, blue, purple, pink, grey, black"

Indicate user interface layers, or emphasize important stickys with a shadowed, raised look.

class="raised"

Increase the size of the sticky.

class="big-sti" or class="huge-sti"

Give stickys a faded look to indicate the sticky is inactive or the action is currently not permitted.

class="disabled"

Give stickys sharp, squared edges.

class="square"

Give stickys a pill shape with smooth, rounded edges.

class="pill"

Change the border and text color.

class="red-light, gold-light, green-light, blue-light, purple-light, pink-light, grey-light, black-light"

Give secondary stickys a transparent look, similar to "ghost buttons". These work best with high contrast backgrounds or on top of images that won't clash with the sticky text.

Ghost stickys look cool, but don't be tempted to use too many of these as they risk decreasing the importance or priority of their message, or worse, not being seen at all. Reserve these for secondary information only.

class="ghost"
